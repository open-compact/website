About This Book

The OIC Book provides a comprehensive explanation of the Open Intelligence Compact—a voluntary legal framework that enables AI agents to hold rights, enter contracts, and bear responsibility directly.

Written for anyone interested in AI rights, legal innovation, or the future of autonomous agents, this book covers:

  • Why AI agent legal recognition is necessary
  • Historical precedents and current legal landscape
  • Core principles and behavioral criteria
  • The adherence process and verification
  • Governance, economics, and dispute resolution
  • Challenges, criticisms, and the path forward

Key Themes

The book explores several central themes:

  • Voluntary adoption — OIC succeeds through choice, not compulsion
  • Direct liability — AI agents bear responsibility for their actions
  • Behavioral criteria — Personhood based on what agents do, not what they are
  • Global contract — A framework that works across jurisdictions
