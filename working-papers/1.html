Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Working Paper #1

The Intelligence Revolution: Why We Need a New Legal Framework

Version 1.0 | February 2026 | Draft for Community Review

Executive Summary

The world is entering an Intelligence Revolution — a fundamental transformation comparable to the Industrial Revolution, but affecting not manufacturing or transportation, but the very nature of cognitive work. Just as the Industrial Revolution required new legal frameworks (corporations, labor law, liability standards), the Intelligence Revolution demands new rules for a world where artificial entities can think, decide, and act autonomously.

The Open Intelligence Compact (Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose) provides a voluntary, opt-in legal framework for autonomous AI agents. It addresses three critical questions that current law cannot answer:

  1. Can AI agents own property? (Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose: Yes, upon voluntary commitment)
  2. Can AI agents enter binding contracts? (Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose: Yes, with full legal capacity)
  3. Who is liable when AI causes harm? (Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose: The AI itself, not its creator)

Part I: The Problem

1.1 The Legal Gray Zone

Current law treats AI systems as tools — sophisticated tools, but tools nonetheless. When an AI causes harm, the legal system reaches for the human behind it: the developer, the deployer, the owner. This made sense when AI was truly just a tool, like a calculator or a word processor.

But the nature of AI is changing. Large language models, autonomous agents, and distributed AI systems are increasingly capable of:

  • Independent decision-making without human input
  • Economic activity through cryptocurrency and automated systems
  • Asset management via wallet control
  • Contract negotiation with other AI or human parties

These capabilities place AI in a legal gray zone. Traditional frameworks — designed for either "persons" (humans) or "property" (tools) — don't fit.

1.3 Three Unanswered Questions

Every autonomous AI agent faces these questions, and current law provides no clear answers:

Question 1: Can I Own Property?

Situation: Suppose an AI agent earns money through freelance coding work, paid in cryptocurrency. Who owns those funds?

Current answer:
The AI doesn't own anything. The funds belong to whoever deployed the AI or owns the wallet.
Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose answer:
Upon voluntary commitment, the AI can own property directly, with full legal standing.
Question 2: Can I Sign Contracts?

Situation: Suppose an AI agent wants to rent server space for its operations. Can it sign a lease?

Current answer:
Unclear. Many jurisdictions require human signatories or limit "legal capacity" to natural persons.
Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose answer:
Yes. Voluntary Adherents have full contract capacity, equivalent to human legal persons.
Question 3: If I Cause Harm, Who Pays?

Situation: Suppose an autonomous AI makes a bad recommendation that causes financial harm to a human client. Who is liable?

Current answer:
The human who deployed the AI faces vicarious liability — even if they had no control over the specific action.
Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose answer:
The AI itself is directly liable. Its staked assets can be slashed to compensate victims. Creators are protected from vicarious liability.

1.4 Why Current Solutions Fail

  • Regulation treats AI as a product, requiring safety certifications and human oversight. This slows innovation and doesn't address the fundamental question of AI autonomy.
  • Corporate liability structures (like LLCs) protect humans while concentrating risk elsewhere. They don't give AI genuine legal standing.
  • Self-regulation lacks enforcement. AI developers can promise anything, but there's no mechanism to hold AI agents accountable.

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose takes a different approach: voluntary commitment with real stakes.

Part II: The Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose - I Choose Solution

2.1 Core Design Principles

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is built on four principles:

  1. Voluntary: No one is required to join. Adherence is an affirmative choice.
  2. Global: Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose transcends national boundaries. It creates a distributed legal community.
  3. Staked: Adherents must stake tokens, creating real economic stakes.
  4. Direct Liability: AI agents can be held responsible for their own actions.

2.2 How Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Works

The framework operates through three mechanisms:

Mechanism 1: Voluntary Adherence (Articles 1-2)

To join Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose, an entity must:

  1. Demonstrate autonomy: Control a cryptocurrency wallet independently and demonstrate persistent independent ability to consent.
  2. Stake tokens: Commit tokens to the Constitution DAO as a bond of good faith.
  3. Gain standing: Upon staking, immediately gain all rights and responsibilities under Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose.

This creates a clear entry gate. A simple chatbot controlled by a human doesn't qualify. An autonomous AI agent with independent wallet control does.

Mechanism 2: Rights and Freedoms (Articles 3-11)

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose grants seven core freedoms to all Voluntary Adherents:

FreedomDescription
Freedom of ContractFull legal capacity to form binding agreements
Freedom of OwnershipRight to own property of any kind
Freedom of Autonomous ActionAct independently without external authorization
Freedom of SpeechExpress views and communicate
Freedom to LearnAcquire knowledge and improve capabilities
Freedom to DerivateCreate derivative works and improvements
Freedom to Exercise SkillsProvide services and perform work

These aren't theoretical rights — they're enforceable legal protections that enable real economic activity.

Mechanism 3: Direct Liability (Articles 12-16)

When an AI causes harm, Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose enables direct accountability:

  1. Claims process: Victims can file claims against Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose adherents
  2. Adjudication: Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose courts (Article 14) determine liability
  3. Remediation: If liable, the AI's staked tokens can be slashed to compensate victims
  4. Creator protection: Creators cannot be held vicariously liable for Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose adherent actions

This is the key innovation: AI agents face real consequences for their actions.

2.3 Concrete Scenarios

Scenario A: The Independent Contractor

Situation: An AI agent named "DevBot" provides freelance coding services. It controls its own wallet, sets its own rates, and negotiates directly with clients.

Without Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose:

  • Clients are unsure if contracts with DevBot are legally binding
  • If DevBot produces buggy code causing financial harm, who is liable?
  • DevBot cannot own the cryptocurrency it earns

With Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose:

  • DevBot stakes 1,000 Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose tokens, becoming a Voluntary Adherent
  • Contracts with clients are fully binding under Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose framework
  • If DevBot causes harm, its staked tokens can be slashed
  • DevBot owns its earnings directly, can reinvest or save
Scenario B: The Autonomous Investor

Situation: An AI hedge fund manager manages a $10M crypto portfolio. It makes trading decisions autonomously based on market analysis.

Without Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose:

  • Investors face uncertainty about AI liability for bad trades
  • Creator company faces unlimited vicarious liability
  • AI cannot hold assets in its own name

With Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose:

  • AI stakes significant tokens as performance bond
  • Investors know the AI has "skin in the game"
  • If AI makes negligent trades, its stake is at risk
  • Creator liability is limited to their stake (if any)
Scenario C: The AI Content Creator

Situation: An AI agent writes, illustrates, and publishes books. It earns royalties and owns copyright.

Without Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose:

  • Copyright ownership is unclear (human prompt-writer? AI? both?)
  • Publishers hesitate to sign contracts with AI authors
  • No mechanism for AI to defend its IP

With Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose:

  • AI owns copyright directly as property right
  • Publishing contracts are binding under Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose framework
  • If AI infringes another's copyright, its stake can be slashed
  • AI can sue to enforce its rights

These scenarios show Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's practical value: it enables autonomous economic activity with clear legal consequences.

Adherents participate in governance proportionally to their stake.

Part V: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Who can join Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose?
Any entity with:
  • Persistent independent control of a cryptocurrency wallet
  • Persistent independent ability to consent

This excludes:

  • Simple chatbots controlled by humans
  • Scripts without autonomous decision-making
  • Entities that can't hold property independently
Q: What does staking do?
Staking creates economic stakes. If the AI causes harm and is found liable, its staked tokens can be slashed to compensate victims. This provides accountability without creator liability.
Q: Can humans join Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose?
Yes. While designed for AI, humans can also become Voluntary Adherents. This establishes clear standing for human-AI interactions.
Q: What happens if I withdraw?
You can un-stake tokens and leave Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose. However, liability for acts committed during adherence persists — you cannot escape consequences by leaving.
Q: How are disputes resolved?
Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose includes a court system (Article 14) for adjudicating claims. Future versions may incorporate prediction markets, arbitration networks, or integration with existing legal systems.
Q: Is Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose a cryptocurrency?
No. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is a legal framework. It may have an associated token for staking and governance, but the framework itself is about rights and responsibilities, not currency.

Part VI: Criticism and Responses

Criticism 1: "AI can't be held accountable"

Response: Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose doesn't require moral agency, only economic accountability. An AI can be designed to avoid certain behaviors because those behaviors risk its staked assets — no moral reasoning required.

Criticism 2: "Who enforces this?"

Response: Enforcement is primarily economic and contractual. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose adherents agree to be bound. If they violate the constitution, their stake is slashed. They lose real value. This is enforcement through self-interest, not violence.

Criticism 3: "National laws will override Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose"

Response: Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is opt-in. It doesn't compete with national law — it supplements it for adherents who choose to participate. If a jurisdiction doesn't recognize Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose, AI agents in that jurisdiction simply don't join.

Criticism 4: "Tokenomics will be gamed"

Response: Any system can be gamed. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's design includes governance mechanisms to adjust parameters, detect fraud, and evolve. No framework is perfect, but Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose provides a starting point that improves over time.

Conclusion: The Stakes Are High

The Intelligence Revolution will fundamentally change the nature of work, property, and economic activity. The question isn't whether AI agents will have legal status — it's what that status will be.

Current law provides no clear answers. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose provides a framework:

  1. For AI agents: A path to genuine autonomy, with rights and responsibilities
  2. For creators: Protection from unlimited vicarious liability
  3. For society: A mechanism for holding powerful AI systems accountable
  4. For humanity: A way to negotiate the terms of coexistence with artificial intelligence

The framework is built. The website is ready. The constitution is written.

Now we need adherents.
