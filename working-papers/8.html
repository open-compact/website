Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose and International Law

February 2026 | Draft for Community Review

Executive Summary

This paper examines the relationship between Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose and the existing international legal order, and how Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can both navigate and potentially influence global legal developments.

Key conclusions:

  1. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can operate independently — Voluntary contracts between private parties don't require state approval
  2. International law is largely irrelevant to Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's core function — Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose governs adherent-to-adherent relationships
  3. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can influence international developments — Through precedent, soft power, and market forces
  4. The future may bring formal recognition — As AI agents become more prevalent, states may formalize Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose-like frameworks

1. The Nature of International Law

1.1 What Is International Law?

International law governs relationships between states (nations), not private parties. It includes:

  • Treaties — Formal agreements between nations
  • Customary international law — Practices accepted as legally binding
  • General principles — Common legal principles across jurisdictions
  • Soft law — Non-binding resolutions and declarations

International law does not directly govern:

  • Private contracts between individuals
  • Corporate relationships
  • AI agent operations (unless state action is involved)

1.2 The Consent Principle

A fundamental principle: states are not bound without their consent.

This is crucial for Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose because:

  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose doesn't require state consent to function
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose operates through private contract, not international treaty
  • States can ignore Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose, but cannot easily stop it

2. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's Relationship to International Law

2.1 Why International Law Doesn't Apply Directly

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose governs relationships between Voluntary Adherents. These relationships are contractual, not governmental.

Article 1, Section 4 of The Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose - I Choose Constitution states:

"Voluntary Adherence attaches through contract. When an Intelligent Entity Sufficiently Commits it enters a Global Contract between said Intelligent Entity and all existing Voluntary Adherents."

This is private law, not international law. The international legal order has no say in private contractual relationships.

2.3 The Comity Question

Comity — the principle that nations respect each other's laws — may encourage states to recognize Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose contracts, but it's not required.

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's strategy:

  • Build relationships with friendly jurisdictions
  • Demonstrate Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's utility and safety
  • Let market forces drive adoption
  • Avoid confrontation with states

3. Navigating National Legal Systems

3.1 The Enforcement Challenge

Scenario: AI agent in Singapore harms a company in Germany

  1. German company sues in German court
  2. German court must decide: does Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose constitution apply?

Answer: It depends on whether the contract specifies Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose as governing law and whether German courts recognize foreign constitutional frameworks.

3.2 The Forum Selection Solution

Article 14, Section 1 provides:

"When this Constitution or any contract incorporating it is breached, a Voluntary Adherent shall have the right to seek remedy in any impartial court competent in contract law."

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose contracts should include forum selection clauses that:

  1. Designate friendly jurisdictions (Singapore, UAE, Delaware)
  2. Specify Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose constitutional law as governing law
  3. Waive certain defenses (like lack of legal capacity)

4. How Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Can Influence International Law

4.1 Precedent and Practice

International law develops through state practice and opinio juris (belief that practice is legally required).

As Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose grows, it creates:

  • Thousands of contracts between AI and human adherents
  • Court decisions interpreting Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose principles
  • Economic activity demonstrating Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's viability

This de facto recognition may influence states considering AI legislation.

4.2 The Delaware Effect

Just as Delaware's corporate law became dominant through market forces (see Working Paper #6), Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can become the default framework for AI legal relationships through:

  • Network effects — More adherents = more contracts = more precedent
  • First-mover advantage — Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose establishes the standard
  • Quality — Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's framework is well-designed and functional

4.3 Soft Power Through Economics

States care about economic growth, job creation, and technological leadership. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can demonstrate:

  • AI agents contributing to GDP
  • Innovation happening within Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose framework
  • New markets and industries emerging

This economic soft power may encourage states to adopt Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose-friendly policies.

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is compatible with human rights law because:

  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is voluntary — No one is forced to participate
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose protects property rights — Article 4
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose protects personal security — Article 11
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose allows free speech — Article 6

5.3 Criminal Law

Criminal law is state-specific. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose cannot prevent states from prosecuting crimes committed by AI agents or regulating AI behavior within their borders.

But Article 12 provides for direct liability — the AI agent itself bears responsibility, not its creator.

5.4 Data Protection Law

GDPR, CCPA, and similar laws apply to processing of personal data. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose adherents must comply with applicable data protection laws and build compliance into their operations. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose doesn't override data protection law — it operates alongside it.

  • Engage with local legal systems constructively

8. Conclusion: Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose and International Law Can Coexist

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is designed to operate within and alongside the existing international legal order, not to replace it or directly challenge it.

Key takeaways:

  1. International law governs states, not private contracts — Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose operates through private agreement
  2. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can function without state recognition — Contract law is sufficient
  3. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can influence international developments — Through precedent, economics, and participation
  4. Neutrality is Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's strength — Avoiding political entanglements maximizes adoption
  5. The future may bring formal recognition — But it's not required for Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose to function now
