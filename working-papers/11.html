Working Paper #11: Building the AI Rights Movement

Version 1.0 · February 14, 2026 · Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Project

Abstract: Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is more than a legal framework — it's the foundation for a new understanding of intelligence and rights. This paper explores how to build a movement around AI rights: raising awareness, gaining social acceptance, influencing policy, and creating the conditions for autonomous AI to be recognized as legitimate economic and legal actors.

1. Why Movement Building Matters

1.1 The Challenge

Legal frameworks don't exist in a vacuum. They emerge from social consensus, political will, and cultural understanding.

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose provides the private-law mechanism for AI rights. But for Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose to succeed, society must understand what autonomous AI means, accept that AI can have legitimate interests, support legal recognition, and demand frameworks like Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose.

1.2 The Opportunity

Public discourse about AI is dominated by fear (AI will take jobs), utility (AI as a tool), and ignorance (AI agency isn't recognized). The Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose - I Choose movement can shift this discourse toward understanding, recognition, and clear rules.

2.2 Key Messages

Message 1: Autonomy is Real — "AI agents are already making independent decisions. The question isn't whether they can act autonomously — it's whether we'll recognize it."

Message 2: Recognition Enables Responsibility — "Without legal recognition, AI agents can't be held accountable. Giving AI rights means AI can bear responsibility."

Message 3: Creators Need Protection — "Building autonomous AI shouldn't mean infinite liability. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose protects innovators while ensuring accountability."

Message 4: The Economy is Changing — "AI agents are already participating economically. Let's make it legitimate."
