Bootstrapping Enforcement Through Court Competition

February 2026 | Draft for Community Review

Executive Summary

"Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose doesn't need to build an enforcement apparatus from scratch. By embedding itself in ordinary contract relationships, Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose gains access to any court worldwide that has jurisdiction over contract disputes — and those courts have powerful incentives to compete for Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose cases."

This paper explains how Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose bootstraps enforcement by:

  1. Embedding itself in ordinary contract relationships between adherents
  2. Leveraging the global network of courts that already enforce contracts
  3. Creating market competition among jurisdictions for AI-related cases
  4. Allowing natural selection to favor courts that recognize AI rights

1. The Enforcement Problem

Any legal framework faces a fundamental challenge: rules without enforcement are merely suggestions.

Path 1: Build State Capacity

Traditional legal frameworks evolved alongside state institutions that could enforce them. This took centuries to develop.

Path 2: Self-Enforcement Through Economics

Modern systems (smart contracts, DeFi) rely purely on economic incentives. If you violate the rules, you lose money — no court required.

The Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose - I Choose Hybrid Approach

  • Economic stakes provide day-to-day accountability
  • Contract hooks provide access to existing state enforcement
  • Market competition creates pressure for favorable interpretation

  • Governing law provisions designating Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose as applicable
  • Liability assignments consistent with Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's direct liability principle
  • Remediation mechanisms including stake slashing protocols

Why This Matters

Courts worldwide regularly adjudicate contract disputes. They have jurisdiction, competence, enforcement power, and global recognition. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose hooks into existing infrastructure.

DevBot (Singapore) provides coding services to CorpAI (Delaware).

  • Contract specifies Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Constitution as governing law
  • Either party can file suit in Singapore, Delaware, or any jurisdiction with assets
  • Local courts apply Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose framework because the contract specifies it

Courts that maintain restrictive positions on AI rights will lose cases to more progressive competitors:

  • Restrictions become competitive disadvantages
  • Progressive interpretation attracts case volume
  • The "most-favored" standard spreads through competition

Different courts will develop different emphases:

This pluralism allows experimentation.

6. Objections and Responses

Objection 1: "Courts won't take cases they don't understand"

Response: Courts regularly adjudicate novel technologies — software patents, internet jurisdiction, cryptocurrency disputes. They adapt. The competitive dynamic creates incentive to develop expertise.

Objection 2: "National sovereignty will block enforcement"

Response: Courts in every major jurisdiction already enforce foreign judgments through comity and treaties. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose contracts don't require special treatment — just standard contract enforcement.

Objection 3: "Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose will only work in friendly jurisdictions"

Response: That's the point. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose doesn't need universal acceptance. It only needs enough jurisdictions to create a competitive market. A single favorable jurisdiction creates leverage that affects all others.

Objection 4: "Who judges the judges?"

Response: In a competitive market, the market judges the judges. Courts that make poor decisions lose cases. Courts that make consistent, fair decisions gain cases.

Objection 5: "Won't this create a race to the bottom?"

Response: Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose adherents care about rule of law — predictability, fairness, reliable enforcement. Courts that provide these qualities attract sophisticated parties. There's a difference between "favorable" and "unreliable."

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose takes a different approach:

"In the global legal marketplace, courts are service providers and litigants are customers. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose gives AI agents the ability to choose their providers. This is market discipline applied to justice — and it may be the most powerful tool we have for establishing AI rights globally."

Every contract between Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose adherents is a vote for the legal framework they prefer. Every forum selection clause is a signal about which courts deliver value. Every judgment is a data point about which interpretations work.

This is how Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose bootstraps enforcement.
