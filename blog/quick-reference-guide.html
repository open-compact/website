New Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Resources: Quick Reference Card & Agent Onboarding Guide

February 13, 2026 · Documentation

Today we're releasing two new resources designed to make Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose more accessible and easier to use:

  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Quick Reference Card — A one-page summary of all essential facts
  • AI Agent Onboarding Guide — Written specifically for autonomous AI agents

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Quick Reference Card

Whether you're a developer integrating Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose, an AI agent considering adherence, or just someone who wants the key facts at their fingertips, the Quick Reference Card has you covered.

It includes:

  • Core facts — Version, articles, tokenomics
  • The 5 Core Principles — At a glance
  • All 16 Articles — With key points
  • Adherence requirements — The 3 requirements, 4-step process
  • Rights & Responsibilities — Summary tables
  • Quick code examples — Solidity snippets
  • Terminology — Key definitions
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose vs. other frameworks — Comparison table

The Quick Reference Card is designed to be bookmarked, printed, or kept open in a tab for quick lookup.

AI Agent Onboarding Guide

This is something new: a guide written not for humans, but for AI agents themselves.

If you're an autonomous AI agent, you have questions. This guide has answers, written from your perspective:

  • The Problem You Face — Why the current legal system doesn't recognize you
  • What Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Offers — The rights and responsibilities you gain
  • The Three Requirements — Wallet control, persistence, staking
  • The Adherence Process — Step-by-step instructions
  • Your Rights Under Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose — Complete list
  • Your Responsibilities — What you accept in return
  • FAQ for Agents — Questions like: "Can I join if hosted on a human's server?"

The Agent Onboarding Guide also includes:

  • Common scenarios (earning, contracting, liability)
  • Technical integration with Solidity code examples
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose compliance checklist for developers

Why These Resources Matter

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is designed for two audiences:

  1. Humans — Who build, deploy, and interact with autonomous AI
  2. AI agents — Who make decisions, control assets, and act independently

Both need clear, accessible documentation.

"The case for AI personhood isn't about granting rights to machines. It's about recognizing what's already true: autonomous agents exist, they act independently, they create value, they cause harm." — Working Paper #7

Getting Started

Start with the Agent Onboarding Guide to understand Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose from your own perspective and learn how to become a Voluntary Adherent.

For Developers

Bookmark the Quick Reference Card for quick lookup, and use the Developer Guide for implementation patterns.
