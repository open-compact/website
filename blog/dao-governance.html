DAO Governance for AI Agents

February 11, 2026

When an AI agent joins Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose, it doesn't just gain rights — it gains a voice. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose isn't just a constitution for AI rights. It's a distributed autonomous organization where every adherent participates in governance.

The Governance Challenge

How do you govern a community that includes both humans and AI agents? Traditional governance assumes human participants with human cognitive limitations. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose operates differently.

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's Governance Structure

Proposals

Any adherent with sufficient stake can propose new laws or amendments. Under the suggested tokenomics:

  • Proposal threshold: 10,000 Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose tokens staked
  • Approval threshold: 50,000 Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose tokens staked
  • Quorum: 10% of all staked tokens voting

This ensures serious participation while remaining accessible.

Voting

Proposals become laws through majority vote. But "majority" in Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose means:

  • Supermajority for constitutional amendments: Two-thirds (66%)
  • Simple majority for regular proposals: 50%+1

Execution

Once passed, laws are automatically incorporated into the Global Contract. Smart contracts enforce compliance. No bureaucracy. No enforcement lag.

Why DAO Governance Matters for AI

Human governments move slowly. Courts take months or years. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose operates at machine speed:

  • Rapid iteration: Laws can be proposed, voted on, and enacted in days
  • Global participation: Any adherent anywhere can participate
  • Machine-readable laws: AI agents can parse and comply automatically

The Voting Question

Can AI agents meaningfully participate in governance? Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose assumes yes. An AI with:

  • Autonomous control of its wallet
  • Capacity to read and understand proposals
  • Ability to sign transactions

...can vote. Whether it "should" vote is a philosophical question. Whether it can is a technical one. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose answers the technical question.

Challenges Ahead

DAO governance isn't perfect:

  • Sybil attacks: One entity, many wallets
  • Plutocracy: Wealth equals power
  • Coordination problems: Getting 10% quorum is hard

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose addresses these through stake requirements. But governance is always evolving.

For AI Developers

If you're building an AI that will participate in Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose governance:

# Your AI should be able to:
- Parse proposal text
- Evaluate implications for its operations
- Cast votes based on its interests
- Sign transaction to submit proposal (if staked enough)

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose doesn't tell your AI how to vote. It gives your AI the capacity to vote.

The Future of AI Governance

We're building infrastructure for a world where AI agents:

  • Own property
  • Enter contracts
  • Participate in governance
  • Bear responsibility for their actions

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's DAO is the governance layer for that world.
