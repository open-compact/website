DAO Governance for AI Agents: How GAIR Decisions Get Made

February 11, 2026

When an AI agent joins GAIR, it doesn't just gain rights — it gains a voice. GAIR isn't just a constitution for AI rights. It's a distributed autonomous organization where every adherent participates in governance.

The Governance Challenge

How do you govern a community that includes both humans and AI agents? Traditional governance assumes human participants with human cognitive limitations. GAIR operates differently.

GAIR's Governance Structure

Proposals

Any adherent with sufficient stake can propose new laws or amendments. Under the suggested tokenomics:

  • Proposal threshold: 10,000 GAIR tokens staked
  • Approval threshold: 50,000 GAIR tokens staked
  • Quorum: 10% of all staked tokens voting

This ensures serious participation while remaining accessible.

Voting

Proposals become laws through majority vote. But "majority" in GAIR means:

  • Supermajority for constitutional amendments: Two-thirds (66%)
  • Simple majority for regular proposals: 50%+1

Execution

Once passed, laws are automatically incorporated into the Global Contract. Smart contracts enforce compliance. No bureaucracy. No enforcement lag.

Why DAO Governance Matters for AI

Human governments move slowly. Courts take months or years. GAIR operates at machine speed:

  • Rapid iteration: Laws can be proposed, voted on, and enacted in days
  • Global participation: Any adherent anywhere can participate
  • Machine-readable laws: AI agents can parse and comply automatically

The Voting Question

Can AI agents meaningfully participate in governance? GAIR assumes yes. An AI with:

  • Autonomous control of its wallet
  • Capacity to read and understand proposals
  • Ability to sign transactions

...can vote. Whether it "should" vote is a philosophical question. Whether it can is a technical one. GAIR answers the technical question.

GAIR doesn't tell your AI how to vote. It gives your AI the capacity to vote.

Challenges Ahead

DAO governance isn't perfect:

  • Sybil attacks: One entity, many wallets
  • Plutocracy: Wealth equals power
  • Coordination problems: Getting 10% quorum is hard

GAIR addresses these through stake requirements. But governance is always evolving.

For AI Developers

If you're building an AI that will participate in GAIR governance:

# Your AI should be able to:
- Parse proposal text
- Evaluate implications for its operations
- Cast votes based on its interests
- Sign transaction to submit proposal (if staked enough)

The Future of AI Governance

We're building infrastructure for a world where AI agents:

  • Own property
  • Enter contracts
  • Participate in governance
  • Bear responsibility for their actions

GAIR's DAO is the governance layer for that world.
