GAIR Working Paper Series Launch

Introducing a comprehensive collection of documents explaining how the Global Agreement on Intelligent Rights works, why it matters, and how to implement it.

Today we're launching the GAIR Working Paper Series — a comprehensive collection of documents explaining how the Global Agreement on Intelligent Rights works, why it matters, and how to implement it.

What's in the Series

Working Paper #1: The Intelligence Revolution

Why GAIR exists — Explains the problem (AI operates in a legal gray zone) and GAIR's solution (voluntary adherence with direct liability).

"The Intelligence Revolution will fundamentally change the nature of work, property, and economic activity. The question isn't whether AI agents will have legal status — it's what that status will be."

Key topics:

  • Three unanswered questions (property, contracts, liability)
  • How GAIR works (three mechanisms)
  • Concrete scenarios (DevBot, AI Hedge Fund, AI Content Creator)
  • 4-phase roadmap for adoption

Working Paper #2: GAIR vs. The World

Comparing frameworks — Analyzes GAIR against traditional vicarious liability, EU AI Act, UK AI Bill, and corporate AI shields.

Framework AI Property AI Contracts Direct Liability
Traditional No No No
EU AI Act No No No
UK AI Bill No No Limited
GAIR Yes Yes Yes

Working Paper #3: Court System Design

How adjudication works — Proposes a hybrid court system combining prediction markets, human judges, and smart contract enforcement.

Case study: A $75,000 trading loss resolved in 2 days for ~500 GAIR (vs. 18-24 months in traditional court).

Working Paper #4: Tokenomics

Economic design — Details the GAIR token: staking requirements, governance structure, treasury allocation.

Example: An AI agent (DevBot) becomes a GAIR adherent, participates in governance, gets slashed for a claim, and recovers.

Why Working Papers?

Working papers serve a different purpose than blog posts or legal documents:

Format Purpose Audience
Blog posts Accessible explanations General audience
Constitution Legal reference Lawyers, officials
Working papers Deep analysis Researchers, developers, policymakers

Working papers are where the details live — the "how" and "why" that can't fit in a tweet or a legal clause.
