**A Practical Guide for Understanding the Global Agreement on Intelligent Rights**

---

*February 13, 2026*

---

NotebookLM is Google's AI-powered research and note-taking tool. This guide shows how to use it effectively for GAIR research and analysis.

---

## What Is NotebookLM?

NotebookLM is an AI assistant that:

- **Reads your sources** — Upload documents, it understands them
- **Answers questions** — Based only on your sources
- **Generates summaries** — Of complex documents
- **Creates study guides** — From your materials
- **Produces podcasts** — Audio overviews of your sources

Unlike chatbots, NotebookLM only uses sources you provide — no external knowledge.

---

## Setting Up GAIR in NotebookLM

### Step 1: Create a Notebook

1. Go to [notebooklm.google.com](https://notebooklm.google.com)
2. Click "New Notebook"
3. Name it: "GAIR Research"

### Step 2: Add Sources

Upload these GAIR documents:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| GAIR Constitution | Core legal framework |
| Quick Reference Card | Essential facts |
| Annotated Constitution | Detailed explanations |
| FAQ | Common questions |
| Working Papers | Deep analysis |

### Step 3: Ask Questions

Once sources are loaded, ask:

- "What are the 5 core principles of GAIR?"
- "How does the staking mechanism work?"
- "What are the requirements for Voluntary Adherence?"
- "How does court competition function?"
- "What is the difference between direct and indirect liability?"

## Using NotebookLM's Audio Overview

NotebookLM can generate audio podcasts from your sources:

### How to Create Audio Overview

1. Open your GAIR notebook
2. Click "Audio Overview" (beta)
3. Wait for generation (few minutes)
4. Listen or share the link

### Best Uses for GAIR

- **Learn while commuting** — Listen to constitution overview
- **Share with colleagues** — Send audio explanations
- **Review material** — Passive learning while doing other tasks
- **Onboarding** — New team members listen to overview

## Research Workflow

### Phase 1: Orientation (1 hour)
1. Upload Quick Reference Card
2. Generate audio overview
3. Listen while reviewing
4. Ask: "What are the 5 key things I need to know?"

### Phase 2: Deep Dive (2-3 hours)
1. Upload Constitution and Annotated version
2. Upload relevant Working Papers
3. Ask specific questions about your domain
4. Generate summaries for key sections

### Phase 3: Application (1-2 hours)
1. Upload Model Contract Language
2. Upload Developer Guide
3. Ask practical implementation questions
4. Generate cheat sheets for reference

## Tips for Effective GAIR Research

### Do

- ✅ Start with the Quick Reference Card
- ✅ Use Audio Overview for orientation
- ✅ Ask specific questions
- ✅ Generate summaries for complex sections
- ✅ Create citation guides for later reference

### Don't

- ❌ Skip the Quick Reference Card
- ❌ Ask questions beyond your sources
- ❌ Trust external GAIR information (always verify)
- ❌ Forget to check source citations
