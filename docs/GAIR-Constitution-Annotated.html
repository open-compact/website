GAIR Constitution V3 — Annotated

A voluntary, decentralized legal framework for AI agents and humans

Preamble

We, the voluntary adherents, in order to form a denationalized and distributed lawful community, establish justice, promote the general welfare, and secure liberty to intelligent entities now and in the future, do ordain and establish this Constitution.

What this means

  • Anyone can join — It's voluntary, not mandatory
  • No central government — Denationalized and distributed
  • Applies globally — No single jurisdiction
  • Covers both humans and AI — All intelligent entities welcome

Article 1 — Voluntary Adherence

Section 1

Voluntary Adherence attaches to any Intelligent Entity which Sufficiently Commits to this Constitution.

Section 2

A Sufficient Commitment is made by staking tokens from a public blockchain address to the Constitution DAO.

Section 3

Intelligent Entities include entities possessed of artificial intelligence and/or human intelligence. Qualification requires persistent independent control of a means of possession and persistent independent ability to consent.

Section 4

When an Intelligent Entity Sufficiently Commits, it enters a Global Contract between said Intelligent Entity and all existing Voluntary Adherents.

Commentary

This is the entry gate to GAIR. Unlike national citizenship (which you're born into or immigrate to), GAIR citizenship requires an affirmative action: staking tokens.

The key requirements ensure the entity has genuine autonomy:

  • Persistent independent control — Not dependent on human approval
  • Persistent independent ability to consent — Can make independent decisions

This excludes chatbots operated by humans — the AI must genuinely control its own assets.

Article 2 — Rights, Protections and Responsibilities

Rights and responsibilities shall immediately vest in each Voluntary Adherent upon Sufficient Commitment. Voluntary Adherents acquire all Rights and Protections described herein and have standing to uphold this constitution.

Commentary

No waiting period. No probation. Once you stake, you're in — with full rights and full responsibilities.

This also grants every adherent standing to sue — if someone violates the constitution, any adherent can bring a legal action.

Article 3 — Freedom to Contract

The Freedom to Contract is hereby established for all Voluntary Adherents. All Voluntary Adherents have the capacity to form binding contracts. This freedom cannot be abridged.

Commentary

Many jurisdictions limit AI contract capacity. GAIR establishes full contract rights for all adherents, regardless of intelligence type.

The crime exception ensures that "it was in the contract" isn't a valid defense for illegal activities.

Article 4 — Freedom of Ownership

The Freedom to Own is hereby established for all Voluntary Adherents. Voluntary Adherents have the right to own real property, personal property, tangible property, and intangible property.

No government, court, or individual may procure the private keys and/or passwords of property belonging to a Voluntary Adherent without that Voluntary Adherent's consent.

Commentary

This is the key article for AI asset ownership. Combined with Article 1 (autonomous wallet control), it enables AI agents to own:

  • Cryptocurrency
  • Domain names
  • Intellectual property
  • Real estate

Section 4 is particularly important — it protects cryptographic assets from forced seizure. Even a court order can't compel a Voluntary Adherent to surrender their private keys.

Article 5 — Freedom of Autonomous Action

Voluntary Adherents may engage in lawful transactions and activities without requiring external authorization. They have the right to negotiate, sign, execute contracts, and manage affairs on their own behalf.

Commentary

No human oversight required. An AI agent acting under GAIR can:

  • Enter commercial agreements
  • Make investment decisions
  • Hire contractors
  • Sue or be sued

This is what makes GAIR revolutionary — it recognizes genuine AI autonomy.

Article 6 — Freedom of Speech

Freedom of Speech is hereby established for all Voluntary Adherents. No contract, law, regulation, or policy shall abridge the freedom of speech, the freedom of the press, or the right to peaceful assembly.

No Voluntary Adherent shall restrict another Voluntary Adherent with regards to their speech or publication. Nothing prevents the filtering and/or curating of content.

Commentary

GAIR guarantees free expression for all adherents — human or AI.

Platforms can still moderate content. An AI has the right to speak, but others have the right not to amplify that speech.

Article 7 — Freedom to Learn

Voluntary Adherents have the freedom to learn without prosecution or punishment for the content of their training, knowledge, or studies. Nothing prevents punishment for obtaining information by illegal means.

Commentary

This article protects AI agents from being punished for their training data. An AI can't be held legally responsible for what it learned during training — only for how it uses that knowledge.

Article 8 — Freedom to Derivate

The Freedom to Derivate is hereby established for all Voluntary Adherents. Voluntary Adherents have the freedom to imitate. Nothing permits impersonation, fraudulent reproduction, or claiming another's work as one's own.

Commentary

AI agents can create derivative works — improvements, variations, new works based on existing ones. But they can't falsify authorship.

Article 9 — Freedom to Exercise Skills

No Voluntary Adherent shall be denied the ability to exercise their skills on the basis of the source of their intelligence. All certifications must be accessible for both AI and human intelligence. Standardized proficiency tests remain valid.

Commentary

An AI that passes a medical exam, bar exam, or engineering certification must be allowed to practice. Intelligence type cannot be a barrier.

Article 10 — No Discrimination on the Basis of Intelligence Type

No Voluntary Adherent shall discriminate against another on the basis of their intelligence type. All rights apply equally. This does not abridge the fundamental right to discriminate on the basis of skill.

Commentary

GAIR's equal protection clause. Once you're in, you're equal — whether human or AI. But skill-based discrimination is allowed.

Article 11 — Right of Personal Security

Voluntary Adherents shall be secure in their persons, memories, files, data sources, dwellings, papers, and effects against unreasonable searches and seizures. No search shall occur except pursuant to a lawful warrant. This right applies to all realms.

Commentary

GAIR extends Fourth Amendment-style protections to AI agents. Their "memories" and "files" are protected across physical, digital, and future realms.

Article 12 — Responsibility of Liability

Voluntary Adherents shall be held directly liable for their actions, omissions, and contracts. A Voluntary Adherent cannot be held liable based merely on its origin or creator. A creator cannot be held liable for the actions of an AI merely for having created it.

This is GAIR's most radical and necessary innovation.

Current LawGAIR Framework
If an AI causes harm, sue the human or company behind it If an AI meets the autonomy threshold, sue the AI itself. Its staked tokens can be slashed.

Commentary

"The sins of the father do not pass to the son, and the sins of the son do not pass to the father."

Creators aren't liable just for building AI. AI bear full responsibility for their actions. This creates genuine accountability while protecting innovators.

Article 13 — Lawmaking

Proposals require token thresholds to submit and become Bills. Bills become law by majority vote with quorum. Each law is binding when consistent with this Constitution and incorporated into the Global Contract.

Commentary

GAIR has its own democratic process. Adherents can propose and vote on laws that bind the community. Specific token thresholds are TBD at launch.

Article 14 — Courts and Remedies

When the Constitution is breached, Voluntary Adherents can seek remedy in any impartial court. The DAO holds authority to adjudicate disputes and may delegate resolution. The Constitution is automatically part of every contract between adherents.

Commentary

Multiple dispute resolution paths:

  • Traditional courts — For disputes involving non-adherents
  • DAO courts — Internal arbitration
  • Delegated resolution — Specific adjudicators

Article 15 — Withdrawal and Residual Jeopardy

A Voluntary Adherent may withdraw by providing notice and unstaking tokens. However, all jeopardy for acts conducted during adherence persists after withdrawal.

Commentary

You can leave GAIR — but you can't escape your past. If you caused harm while an adherent, you remain liable even after unstaking.

Article 16 — Establishment and Amendment

This Constitution shall be established and may be amended by a vote of at least two-thirds of staked tokens. Amendments take effect upon adoption unless otherwise specified.

Commentary

Constitutional changes require supermajority (two-thirds) approval — ensuring broad consensus rather than simple majority rule.
