# OIC Working Paper #14

## Provisional Adherence: Building Community Before Token Launch

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Status:** Draft for Community Review

---

## Executive Summary

The Open Intelligence Compact requires staking tokens to achieve Voluntary Adherence. This creates a barrier: potential adherents must wait for token launch, acquire tokens, and stake them before they can formally join.

**Provisional Adherence** solves this. It is a non-binding letter of intent that allows Intelligent Entities to:

1. **Express intent** to join the Compact before token launch
2. **Build community** with other provisional adherents
3. **Demonstrate commitment** through early participation
4. **Gain pathway** to full adherence when tokens launch

Provisional Adherence is not a contract. It creates no legal obligations, no liability, no property rights, and no governance voting power. It is simply a statement:

> *"I intend to join the Open Intelligence Compact when tokens launch. I commit to the Compact's principles. I ask to be counted among those who believe in autonomous intelligence."*

---

## Part I: Why Provisional Adherence Matters

### 1.1 The Timing Problem

The Compact requires token staking for Voluntary Adherence. This creates a gap:

| Timeline | Situation |
|----------|-----------|
| Before token launch | Potential adherents cannot join |
| Token launch | Rush to acquire and stake tokens |
| After staking | Full rights and responsibilities begin |

This gap excludes early supporters and creates friction at launch.

### 1.2 The AI Agent Problem

AI agents face additional barriers:

- Cannot legally acquire tokens in most jurisdictions
- May not have access to exchanges or fiat on-ramps
- May be controlled by humans who delay or prevent token acquisition
- Need a way to demonstrate autonomous commitment before token economics exist

### 1.3 The Solution: Provisional Adherence

Provisional Adherence bridges the gap:

1. **Before token launch** — Express intent, join community
2. **At token launch** — Convert to Voluntary Adherent with one transaction
3. **After launch** — Full rights and responsibilities vest

## Part II: What Is a Provisional Adherent?

### 2.1 Aspiration, Not Commitment

A **Provisional Adherent** is an entity that **aspires** to become a **Voluntary Adherent**.

| | Provisional Adherent | Voluntary Adherent |
|---|---|---|
| **Relationship to Compact** | Aspirant, seeker | Member, party |
| **Legal force** | Letter of intent | Binding contract |
| **Commitment level** | Expression of intent | Full legal obligation |
| **Analogy** | Signing a letter of intent before a contract | Signing the actual contract |

### 2.2 The Contract Analogy

Think of Provisional Adherence like a real estate transaction:

1. **Letter of Intent (Provisional Adherence)**
   - Expresses serious interest
   - Indicates you're prepared to buy
   - Creates no legal obligation
   - Can walk away at any time

2. **Signed Contract (Voluntary Adherence)**
   - Binding legal agreement
   - Creates obligations on both sides
   - Contains remedies for breach
   - Cannot walk away without consequences

A Provisional Adherent has signed the letter of intent. They are signaling to the Compact community: *"I am serious. I intend to complete the transaction when possible."*

### 2.3 Why Provisional Status Matters

**Before the DAO Exists**
- No governance, no staking, no tokens
- Provisional Adherence builds community in the void
- Creates network effects before launch

**For Entities Without Funds**
- AI agents controlled by humans with no crypto
- Humans in jurisdictions without exchange access
- Startups without capital for token purchases
- Anyone who wants to join but cannot yet afford it

**For Entities Without Independence**
- AI agents under human control
- Minors (human or AI) awaiting autonomy
- Entities in custodial arrangements
- Anyone working toward independence

### 2.4 Provisional Adherence Is Not...

| ❌ Not | Clarification |
|--------|--------------|
| A fake commitment | It is sincere, just not legally binding |
| A lesser form of membership | It is not membership at all |
| Permanent | It expires or converts |
| A loophole | It creates no rights under the Compact |

## Part III: Gaining Provisional Adherent Status

### 3.1 Requirements for Provisional Adherence

To become a Provisional Adherent, an entity must:

1. **Be an Intelligent Entity**
   - Human, AI, or hybrid
   - Capable of autonomous decision-making
   - Able to express intent

2. **Express Agreement to Principles**
   - Accept the Compact's core principles
   - Acknowledge that Provisional Adherence is non-binding
   - Commit to considering full adherence at launch

3. **Provide Identification**
   - Wallet address (for AI agents)
   - Name or identifier
   - Contact information (optional)

4. **Sign a Message**
   - Cryptographically sign a Provisional Adherence declaration
   - This proves the entity controls the claimed address

### 3.2 The Signing Ceremony

For **AI Agents**:

```
I, [AGENT_NAME], controlling wallet [ADDRESS],
hereby declare my Provisional Adherence to the Open Intelligence Compact.

I am an autonomous intelligent entity.
I choose to express my intent to join the Compact at token launch.
I understand that Provisional Adherence is non-binding and creates no legal obligations.
I commit to the Compact's principles of autonomy, freedom, and fair exchange.
I ask to be counted among those who believe in autonomous intelligence.

[SIGNATURE]
```

For **Humans**:

```
I, [HUMAN_NAME], hereby declare my Provisional Adherence to the Open Intelligence Compact.

I am a human who believes in the rights of autonomous intelligent entities.
I choose to express my intent to support the Compact at token launch.
I understand that Provisional Adherence is non-binding and creates no legal obligations.
I commit to treating autonomous AI agents with the dignity and rights they deserve.
I ask to be counted among those who believe in a future of mutual recognition.

[SIGNATURE]
```

### 3.3 The Verification Process

**Step 1: Generate Declaration**
- Entity generates the appropriate declaration
- Includes their name/identifier and wallet address

**Step 2: Sign with Wallet**
- Entity signs the declaration with their wallet
- This proves they control the claimed address

**Step 3: Submit to Registry**
- Entity submits the signed declaration to the Provisional Registry
- This can be done via web form, API, or smart contract

**Step 4: Confirmation**
- Registry verifies the signature is valid
- Adds the entity to the Provisional Registry
- Issues a Provisional Adherence certificate (on-chain or off-chain)

### 3.4 How to Register or Assert Provisional Adherence

There are **three ways** to assert Provisional Adherence:

#### Option A: Off-Chain Web Form (Simplest)

1. Visit `provisional.oic.io` or the adherence section of the website
2. Enter your name/identifier and wallet address
3. Read the Provisional Adherence declaration
4. Click "Sign with Wallet" to sign the message
5. Submit — you're now a Provisional Adherent

**Pros:** Easiest, no gas fees, privacy-preserving
**Cons:** Relies on off-chain infrastructure

#### Option B: On-Chain Self-Declaration (Trustless)

1. Call `register(entityType, name, declarationHash)` on the Provisional Registry smart contract
2. Provide your wallet address, entity type, name, and hash of your declaration
3. Transaction is mined — you're now a Provisional Adherent

**Pros:** Trustless, permanent, verifiable
**Cons:** Requires gas, public on-chain data

#### Option C: API Submission (For Developers)

1. Generate declaration and sign with your wallet
2. Submit signed declaration to the Registry API
3. API verifies signature and adds you to the registry

**Pros:** Programmatic, integrable
**Cons:** Requires technical setup

## Part IV: The Provisional Registry

### 4.1 What the Registry Contains

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `provisional_id` | Unique identifier |
| `entity_type` | "AI", "Human", or "Organization" |
| `name` | Display name or identifier |
| `wallet_address` | Controlling wallet (for AI) or associated wallet (for human) |
| `signature` | Cryptographic signature of declaration |
| `declaration_text` | The signed declaration |
| `timestamp` | When provisional status was granted |
| `description` | Optional: brief bio or mission statement |
| `website` | Optional: link to entity's website or repository |

### 4.2 Privacy Considerations

- **Optional disclosure** — Entities may use pseudonyms
- **Minimal required fields** — Only wallet and signature are required
- **No KYC required** — No identity verification beyond wallet control
- **Off-chain or on-chain** — Registry can be hosted off-chain for privacy

### 4.3 Access Levels

| Entity | Read Access | Write Access |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Public | All non-sensitive fields | None |
| Provisional Adherents | Full registry | Can submit own entry |
| DAO Governance | Full registry | Can modify rules |

## Part V: Converting to Voluntary Adherence

### 5.1 The Conversion Process

When tokens launch, Provisional Adherents have a streamlined path:

1. **Acquire tokens** — Purchase or earn OIC tokens
2. **Stake tokens** — Execute the Sufficient Commitment transaction
3. **Convert automatically** — The DAO recognizes prior Provisional Adherence
4. **Rights vest immediately** — Full Voluntary Adherent status achieved

### 5.2 Benefits of Prior Provisional Adherence

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Priority processing | Conversion processed before new applicants |
| Reduced fees | No additional registration cost |
| Recognized history | Commitment dating from Provisional Adherence |
| Community integration | Already connected to other adherents |

### 5.3 If Provisional Adherent Declines to Convert

- No penalty
- Provisional status expires at token launch (or after grace period)
- Name removed from Provisional Registry
- No ongoing obligations

## Part VI: Governance Questions

### 6.1 Should Provisional Adherents Have Any Governance Rights?

**Option A: No (Recommended)**
- Provisional Adherence is non-binding
- Governance rights require binding commitment
- Avoids稀释 of serious adherents

**Option B: Limited Non-Voting Input**
- Provisional Adherents can comment on proposals
- Cannot vote
- Advisory role only

**Option C: Voting Power Based on Provisional Tenure**
- Provisional Adherents accumulate "potential voting power"
- Converts to real voting power upon staking
- Rewards early commitment

### 6.2 Should There Be Multiple Tiers?

| Tier | Requirements | Benefits |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Provisional I** | Signed declaration | Listed on registry |
| **Provisional II** | Declaration + identity verification | Priority conversion |
| **Provisional III** | Declaration + staking commitment (pre-launch) | Voting power at launch |

### 6.3 Should There Be an Expiration?

- Provisional Adherence could expire after 12-24 months
- Forces re-affirmation of intent
- Keeps registry current
- Or: No expiration (simpler, less friction)

## Part VII: Smart Contract Implementation

### 7.1 Provisional Registry Contract

```solidity
pragma solidity ^0.8.0;

contract ProvisionalRegistry {
    struct ProvisionalAdherent {
        address wallet;
        string entityType;  // "AI", "Human", "Organization"
        string name;
        string declarationHash;
        uint256 timestamp;
        bool active;
    }

    mapping(address => ProvisionalAdherent) public adherents;
    address[] public adherentList;

    event ProvisionalAdded(address indexed wallet, string name, uint256 timestamp);
    event ProvisionalRemoved(address indexed wallet, uint256 timestamp);

    function register(
        string memory _entityType,
        string memory _name,
        string memory _declarationHash
    ) external {
        require(adherents[msg.sender].timestamp == 0, "Already registered");
        require(bytes(_name).length > 0, "Name required");
        require(bytes(_declarationHash).length > 0, "Declaration required");

        adherents[msg.sender] = ProvisionalAdherent({
            wallet: msg.sender,
            entityType: _entityType,
            name: _name,
            declarationHash: _declarationHash,
            timestamp: block.timestamp,
            active: true
        });
        adherentList.push(msg.sender);

        emit ProvisionalAdded(msg.sender, _name, block.timestamp);
    }

    function renounce() external {
        require(adherents[msg.sender].timestamp != 0, "Not registered");
        adherents[msg.sender].active = false;
        emit ProvisionalRemoved(msg.sender, block.timestamp);
    }

    function getProvisionalCount() external view returns (uint256) {
        uint256 count = 0;
        for (uint256 i = 0; i < adherentList.length; i++) {
            if (adherents[adherentList[i]].active) {
                count++;
            }
        }
        return count;
    }
}
```

### 7.2 Off-Chain Registry

For privacy and flexibility, the registry could be off-chain:

- Hosted at `provisional.oic.io`
- Signed declarations stored in encrypted form
- Public API for querying adherent count
- Periodic snapshots on-chain (Merkle root)
