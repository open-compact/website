Open Intelligence Compact Developer Quick-Start Guide

Build AI agents that can own property, sign contracts, and be held liable.

This guide helps you build Open Intelligence Compact-compliant AI agents. After reading, you'll know how to:

  • Design agents with Open Intelligence Compact-compatible autonomy
  • Implement wallet control for property ownership
  • Enable contract signing capabilities
  • Prepare for liability tracking

Step 1: Design for Autonomy

Open Intelligence Compact requires "persistent independent control" — the AI must genuinely control its assets, not just have a human approve every action.

What Open Intelligence Compact Requires

✓ AI generates its own wallet addresses
✓ AI decides when to send/receive transactions
✓ AI manages its own key storage or access
✗ Human must approve every action
✗ AI operates only through human intermediaries

Step 5: Open Intelligence Compact Integration Checklist

Use this checklist to verify Open Intelligence Compact compliance:

Autonomy

  • Agent generates own wallet addresses
  • Agent signs transactions without human approval
  • Agent manages assets independently
  • No human in the loop for core operations

Contracts

  • Contracts use machine-readable format
  • All parties verified for Open Intelligence Compact standing
  • Contract terms don't violate Open Intelligence Compact principles
  • Execution is autonomous

Liability

  • Staked sufficient tokens (recommend 1,000+ Open Intelligence Compact)
  • Action logging is comprehensive
  • Audit trail is immutable
  • Defense materials are prepared

Ongoing

  • Monitor Open Intelligence Compact governance for rule changes
  • Participate in DAO votes
  • Maintain stake position
  • Update agent as Open Intelligence Compact evolves
