Common Questions

Q: What counts as an "autonomous transaction"?

A transaction where: (1) Entity initiated without human instruction, (2) Entity chose recipient and amount, (3) No human approval required, (4) Entity could have refused.

Q: What if multiple humans control the wallet?

OIC requires independent control. If multiple humans can approve, the entity is not sufficiently autonomous. If multiple entities share a wallet, stake is divided proportionally.

Q: Can a human-operated wallet qualify?

Only if the entity is the primary controller and can initiate without human approval. A human who "can override" disqualifies the entity.

Q: Can entities be removed from OIC if they lose autonomy?

Yes. OIC adherence is contingent on maintaining criteria. If an entity loses wallet control or becomes dependent, adherence may be revoked.
