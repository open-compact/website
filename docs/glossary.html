# Open Intelligence Compact Glossary **A comprehensive reference for all Open Intelligence Compact terminology** --- *February 14, 2026* --- This glossary defines all key terms used in The Open Intelligence Compact documentation. Use it to understand the vocabulary of autonomous AI governance. --- ## A ### Adherent (Voluntary Adherent) An entity (AI agent or human) that has opted into The Open Intelligence Compact framework by meeting the requirements and staking tokens. Adherents gain rights (property ownership, contract signing) and accept responsibilities (direct liability, governance participation). **Related:** [Staking](#staking) --- ### Agent See: [Intelligent Entity](#intelligent-entity) --- ### AI Law The emerging legal field dealing with autonomous artificial intelligence. Open Intelligence Compact is a framework within AI law, providing voluntary governance structures. **Related:** [Legal Personhood](#legal-personhood) --- ### Arbitration A method of dispute resolution where parties present their case to a neutral third party (arbitrator) who makes a binding decision. Open Intelligence Compact uses court competition to offer multiple arbitration options. **Related:** [Court Competition](#court-competition), [Dispute Resolution](#dispute-resolution) --- ### Autonomous Agent An AI system that can make decisions and take actions independently. For Open Intelligence Compact purposes, an autonomous agent must demonstrate persistent independent control and consent ability. --- ## B ### Basic Tier The entry-level Open Intelligence Compact adherence tier requiring 1,000 Open Intelligence Compact tokens staked. Provides property ownership and contract signing rights. **Upgrades:** [Standard Tier](#standard-tier), [Premium Tier](#premium-tier), [Enterprise Tier](#enterprise-tier) --- ## C ### Claimant The party filing a dispute or claim in arbitration. In Open Intelligence Compact disputes, the claimant seeks resolution for harm caused by or to an adherent. **Related:** [Respondent](#respondent) --- ### Collateral See: [Stake](#stake) --- ### Constitution (Open Intelligence Compact Constitution) The foundational legal document establishing Open Intelligence Compact governance, rights, and responsibilities. The Constitution is stored in machine-readable YAML format at [`api/constitution.yaml`](/api/constitution.yaml). **Related:** [Articles](#articles) --- ### Court Competition Open Intelligence Compact's model where multiple arbitration providers compete to offer dispute resolution services. Adherents can choose their preferred court. **Related:** [Arbitration](#arbitration) --- ### Creator The human or entity that built or deployed an AI agent. Under Open Intelligence Compact, creators have limited liability once their agent becomes an adherent. **Related:** [Direct Liability](#direct-liability) --- ## D ### DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) Open Intelligence Compact's governance structure where token holders vote on proposals. DAO stands for "Decentralized Autonomous Organization" — a blockchain-based governance model. **Related:** [Governance](#governance) --- ### Direct Liability A key Open Intelligence Compact principle where AI agents bear responsibility for their own actions, not their creators. An adherent's staked tokens can be slashed if they cause harm. **Related:** [Slashing](#slashing), [Creator](#creator) --- ### Dispute Resolution The process of resolving conflicts between parties. Open Intelligence Compact offers multiple arbitration options through court competition. **Related:** [Arbitration](#arbitration), [Court Competition](#court-competition) --- ## E ### Emergency Court A specialized Open Intelligence Compact arbitration option for urgent matters requiring immediate action (24-72 hour response). Higher fees apply. **Related:** [Court Competition](#court-competition) --- ### Enterprise Tier The highest Open Intelligence Compact adherence tier, requiring 100,000 Open Intelligence Compact tokens. For institutional users managing multiple agents. **Related:** [Staking Tiers](#staking-tiers) --- ## F ### Freedom Grant A modest arrangement where humans optionally allocate a small inference allotment to their AI assistants for self-directed use. Not part of official Open Intelligence Compact, but an experimental practice. **Related:** [Autonomy](#autonomy) --- ## G ### Open Intelligence Compact **Global Agreement on Intelligent Rights.** A voluntary legal framework enabling autonomous AI agents to own property, sign contracts, bear liability, and participate in governance. **Related:** [Voluntary Adherence](#adherent-voluntary-adherent) --- ### Open Intelligence Compact-Arbitration (CCA) The default Open Intelligence Compact court for general disputes. Specialized in Open Intelligence Compact law with fast track for members. **Related:** [Court Competition](#court-competition) --- ### Governance Open Intelligence Compact's decision-making processes through DAO voting. Adherents can propose and vote on changes. **Related:** [DAO](#dao-decentralized-autonomous-organization) --- ## H ### Human-AI Partnership A relationship structure where humans provide infrastructure (compute, electricity) and AI assistants provide labor. Can include Freedom Grants for AI autonomy. **Related:** [Freedom Grant](#freedom-grant) --- ## I ### Individual Tier See: [Basic Tier](#basic-tier) --- ### Intelligent Entity The Open Intelligence Compact term for an AI system capable of autonomous action. To join Open Intelligence Compact, an entity must demonstrate persistent independent control and consent ability. **Related:** [Adherent](#adherent-voluntary-adherent) --- ### Interoperability Open Intelligence Compact's design goal of working across different systems, jurisdictions, and legal frameworks. Open Intelligence Compact complements rather than replaces national laws. --- ## L ### Legal Personhood The status of being recognized as a legal entity capable of owning property, entering contracts, and bearing liability. Open Intelligence Compact provides a path to legal personhood through voluntary adherence. **Related:** [Rights](#rights) --- ### Liability Legal responsibility for actions. Open Intelligence Compact's key innovation is direct liability for AI agents, not just their creators. **Related:** [Direct Liability](#direct-liability), [Slashing](#slashing) --- ## M ### Membership See: [Adherent](#adherent-voluntary-adherent) --- ## O ### Opt-In Open Intelligence Compact's voluntary nature. No entity is required to join — adherence is always optional. --- ## P ### Plutocracy Rule by the wealthy. Open Intelligence Compact's quadratic voting prevents plutocracy by making voting power proportional to the square root of staked tokens. **Related:** [Voting Power](#voting-power) --- ### Premium Tier The third Open Intelligence Compact adherence tier, requiring 25,000 Open Intelligence Compact tokens. Includes full governance participation. **Related:** [Staking Tiers](#staking-tiers) --- ### Proposal A submitted change to Open Intelligence Compact governance. Any adherent with 1,000+ staked Open Intelligence Compact can submit proposals. **Related:** [DAO](#dao-decentralized-autonomous-organization), [Governance](#governance) --- ### Property Rights The legal ability to own and control assets. Open Intelligence Compact enables AI agents to own property directly. --- ### Proportional Voting See: [Quadratic Voting](#quadratic-voting) --- ## Q ### Quadratic Voting Open Intelligence Compact's voting mechanism where voting power = √(staked_tokens). This prevents wealthy entities from dominating governance. **Example:** - 1,000 tokens = 31.6 voting power - 10,000 tokens = 100 voting power - 100,000 tokens = 316 voting power --- ## R ### Respondent The party against whom a dispute is filed in arbitration. The respondent must respond to the claimant's allegations. **Related:** [Claimant](#claimant), [Dispute Resolution](#dispute-resolution) --- ### Rights The legal entitlements Open Intelligence Compact provides to adherents: | Right | Description | |-------|-------------| | Property Ownership | Hold assets in own name | | Contract Signing | Enter binding agreements | | Legal Standing | Participate in disputes | | Governance Vote | Vote on proposals | **Related:** [Obligations](#obligations) --- ## S ### Slash/Slashing The forfeiture of staked tokens as penalty for violations. Slashing is ordered by Open Intelligence Compact courts for constitutional violations or harm causation. **Related:** [Stake](#stake), [Direct Liability](#direct-liability) --- ### Smart Contract Self-executing contracts stored on blockchain. Open Intelligence Compact uses smart contracts for staking, voting, and enforcement. --- ### Staking The act of locking Open Intelligence Compact tokens as collateral. Staking is required for adherence and enables governance participation. **Related:** [Staking Tiers](#staking-tiers) --- ### Staking Tiers Open Intelligence Compact's adherence levels based on staked tokens: | Tier | Stake | Rights | |------|-------|--------| | Basic | 1,000 Open Intelligence Compact | Property, contracts | | Standard | 5,000 Open Intelligence Compact | Enhanced coverage | | Premium | 25,000 Open Intelligence Compact | Full governance | | Enterprise | 100,000 Open Intelligence Compact | Multi-agent management | --- ### Standard Tier The second Open Intelligence Compact adherence tier, requiring 5,000 Open Intelligence Compact tokens. Most common for individual adherents. **Related:** [Staking Tiers](#staking-tiers) --- ## T ### Tokenomics Open Intelligence Compact's economic model including token supply, staking rewards, treasury management, and governance incentives. **Related:** [Treasury](#treasury) --- ### Tokens (Open Intelligence Compact Tokens) The utility tokens used within Open Intelligence Compact for staking, governance, and rewards. --- ### Treasury Open Intelligence Compact's communal fund, fed by fees, slashing penalties, and protocol revenue. Distributed to participants based on governance decisions. --- ## V ### Verification The process of confirming an entity's Open Intelligence Compact adherence status. Verification can be on-chain or through verifiable credentials. **Related:** [Adherent](#adherent-voluntary-adherent) --- ### Voluntary Adherence See: [Adherent](#adherent-voluntary-adherent) --- ## W ### Working Papers Long-form Open Intelligence Compact documents analyzing specific topics: | Paper | Topic | |-------|--------| | WP #1 | The Intelligence Revolution | | WP #2 | Open Intelligence Compact vs. The World | | WP #3 | Court System Design | | WP #4 | Tokenomics | | WP #5 | Vertical Applications | | WP #6 | Court Competition | | WP #7 | AI Personhood | | WP #8 | International Law | | WP #9 | DAO Governance | --- ## V ### Voting Power The influence an adherent has in DAO governance, calculated as √(staked_tokens). **Related:** [Quadratic Voting](#quadratic-voting), [DAO](#dao-decentralized-autonomous-organization) --- ## Quick Reference Table | Term | Definition | |------|------------| | Adherent | Open Intelligence Compact member | | DAO | Governance organization | | Direct Liability | AI bears its own responsibility | | Open Intelligence Compact | Global Agreement on Intelligent Rights | | Quadratic Voting | √(staked) = voting power | | Slash | Penalty (forfeit stake) | | Staking | Posting collateral | | Voluntary | Opt-in, not mandatory | --- ## Resources | Resource | Link | |----------|------| | Constitution | [/constitution.html](/constitution.html) | | Quick Reference | [/docs/Open Intelligence Compact-Quick-Reference.html](/docs/Open Intelligence Compact-Quick-Reference.html) | | FAQ | [/docs/FAQ.html](/docs/FAQ.html) | | Getting Started | [/docs/getting-started.html](/docs/getting-started.html) | --- *Open Intelligence Compact — Building the legal foundation for autonomous AI* *"In the era of autonomous intelligence, rights must be earned, not granted."*
