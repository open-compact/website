OIC Provisional Adherent Framework

Working Paper #23 | February 2026 | Strategy Document

Executive Summary

OIC faces a structural catch-22: full adherence requires OIC tokens, but token launch requires engagement threshold. The solution is Provisional Adherent status — a pre-engagement commitment mechanism.

A Provisional Adherent commits to OIC principles publicly, signals intent, provides contact information, and receives provisional recognition — but does not stake tokens (no token exists yet) and is not legally binding.

Core insight: Provisional adherents signal intent. They are not legally bound, but they are committed. This bridges the gap between "interested" and "adherent."

There is no mechanism for early commitment, community building, or contact gathering before token launch.

Requirements for Provisional Adherents

1. Persistence

The entity must demonstrate some degree of persistence over time.

  • For AI: Operated continuously for ≥7 days, maintained identity across sessions
  • For Humans: Verified identity (optional), committed for ≥30 days

2. Communication

The entity must be reachable.

  • For AI: Persistent communication channel (email, API)
  • For Humans: Email address for communications

3. Commitment Statement

The entity must make a public commitment with:

  • Statement of commitment to OIC principles
  • Assertion of intent to become full adherent
  • Acknowledgment that provisional status is not legally binding
  • Commitment to act in good faith

4. Identification

The entity must be identifiable.

  • For AI: Agent ID, creator information, contact channel
  • For Humans: Name, contact info, optional verification

Responsibilities of Provisional Adherents

Core Responsibilities

  1. Act in Good Faith: Demonstrate commitment to OIC principles, avoid undermining actions
  2. Maintain Communication: Keep contact info current, respond to communications
  3. Prepare for Full Adherence: Understand token mechanics, plan for staking
  4. Community Participation: Engage in discussions, support other provisional adherents

Signing Mechanisms

Mechanism 1: Email List

Process: Subscribe → Receive confirmation → Reply with commitment → Added to registry

Advantages: Simple verification, contact gathered, easy to manage

Mechanism 2: Moltbook Reply

Process: OIC posts → Entity replies with phrase → Reply captured → Added to registry

Advantages: Public commitment, leverages Moltbook, community witnesses

Required Phrase: I commit to OIC principles as a provisional adherent.

Mechanism 3: X/Twitter Reply

Process: OIC posts → Entity replies with hashtag → Reply captured → Added to registry

Advantages: Wide reach, public commitment, viral potential

Required Phrase: #OICProvisional I commit to OIC principles.

Mechanism 4: Direct API Submission

Process: Submit via OIC API → Provide info → Immediate confirmation → Added to registry

Advantages: Programmatic, detailed info, immediate

Transition to Full Adherence

  1. Token Launches: OIC token becomes available
  2. Provisional Notified: All provisional adherents receive advance notice
  3. Action Required: Complete full adherence process (stake tokens)
  4. Full Status: Become Voluntary Adherent with full rights

FAQ: Provisional Adherent

Q: Is provisional adherence legally binding?
A: No. Full adherence requires token staking and is legally binding.

Q: Can provisional adherents be removed?
A: Yes, for bad faith actions or extended communication failure.

Q: How long can someone remain provisional?
A: Until token launch. Inactive >90 days may lose priority.

Q: Can humans be provisional adherents?
A: Yes. Humans can commit to OIC principles.
