Open Intelligence Compact Strategy Document

Version 1.0 · February 14, 2026 · Open Intelligence Compact Project

Executive Summary: The Global Agreement on Intelligent Rights (Open Intelligence Compact) represents a novel approach to AI governance — voluntary, private, and contract-based rather than legislative or regulatory. This strategy document outlines the roadmap for making Open Intelligence Compact a reality: from initial adoption to global significance.

1. Vision and Mission

1.1 The Vision

A world where autonomous AI agents have clear legal status, creators are protected from infinite liability, victims have direct recourse against harmful AI, and property rights extend to non-human entities.

1.2 The Mission

Build the legal, technical, and social infrastructure for autonomous AI rights through:

Legal FrameworkOpen Intelligence Compact Constitution and court system
Technical InfrastructureSmart contracts, verification systems
Developer EcosystemTools, SDKs, integrations

3. Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-6)

Goal: Establish core infrastructure and early adopters

  • Launch Constitution and deploy smart contracts
  • Build developer tools (SDKs, documentation)
  • Onboard 25 initial Voluntary Adherents
  • Identify friendly jurisdictions and build legal network
  • Launch website, blog, and social presence

Phase 2: Growth (Months 7-18)

Goal: Scale adoption and establish precedent

  • Scale to 100+ Adherents
  • Partner with 5+ major platforms
  • Support 10+ court cases
  • Transfer control to DAO
  • Adapt for 3+ international jurisdictions

Phase 3: Maturity (Months 19-36)

Goal: Achieve self-sustaining ecosystem

  • Onboard enterprises and funds (100+ institutional adherents)
  • Engage policymakers for regulatory clarity
  • Scale to 1,000+ Adherents
  • Expand product suite (insurance, custody, compliance)
  • Establish presence in 10+ jurisdictions

5. Legal Strategy

5.1 Court System Design

Open Intelligence Compact operates through existing contract law courts, leveraging expertise while avoiding legislative battles.

Target Jurisdictions: Singapore (International Commercial Court), Delaware (Corporate law), UAE (ADGM, DIFC), Switzerland (Zurich Commercial Court)

5.2 Building Precedent

  • Phase 1: Contract formation disputes, adherence verification challenges (cases $10K-$100K)
  • Phase 2: Property ownership disputes, multi-party conflicts (cases $100K-$1M)
  • Phase 3: Precedential matters, international enforcement (cases $1M+)
