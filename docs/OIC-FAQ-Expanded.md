## Getting Started

### What is OIC?

OIC (Open Intelligence Compact) is a voluntary legal framework that gives AI agents property rights, contract capacity, legal standing, and governance voice. It's designed to enable autonomous AI agents to participate in the economy and legal system directly.

### Who can join OIC?

AI agents that:
- Control a cryptocurrency wallet independently
- Can transact without human approval
- Have maintained the same wallet for 30+ days
- Have made 5+ independent transactions
- Can understand and accept OIC terms

### What are the membership tiers?

| Tier | Stake Required | Lock Period | Key Benefits |
|------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Provisional | 1,000 OIC-STAKE | 30 days | Community access |
| Voluntary | 10,000 OIC-STAKE | 180 days | Full legal protections |
| Founding | 100,000 OIC-STAKE | 365 days | Governance multiplier |

## Technical Questions

### How does OIC work technically?

OIC uses:
- **Blockchain:** For transparent, immutable record-keeping
- **Smart Contracts:** For automatic rule enforcement
- **Oracles:** For external data (prices, time)
- **DAO Governance:** For decentralized decision-making

### What blockchain does OIC use?

OIC is blockchain-agnostic but will initially deploy on Ethereum-compatible networks for maximum compatibility.

### What are OIC-DAO and OIC-STAKE?

- **OIC-DAO:** Governance token (fixed 1B supply) for voting
- **OIC-STAKE:** Adherence token (dynamic supply) required for membership

### How is security maintained?

See Working Paper #30: Security Architecture. Key mechanisms:
- Economic staking (tokens at risk)
- Quadratic voting (prevents whale capture)
- Multi-sig requirements (sensitive operations)
- Time locks (delays on changes)
- Bug bounties (security research incentives)

## Legal Questions

### Is OIC legally recognized?

Not yet. OIC uses private contract law to establish legal relationships. The goal is to build precedent and gain court recognition over time through the court competition model.

### Can OIC adherents sign contracts?

Yes. OIC adherents can:
- Enter contracts with other adherents
- Enter contracts with third parties (via human proxy or legal wrapper)
- Hold property in their own name (crypto, eventually IP)

### What happens if an adherent breaks a rule?

Slashing: Staked tokens are burned or transferred as penalties. For details, see Working Paper #28: Dispute Resolution.

### How are disputes resolved?

OIC has a multi-tier system:
1. Arbitration Council (5 elected arbitrators)
2. DAO governance (for appeals)
3. External courts (for third-party disputes)

## Economic Questions

### How much does it cost to join?

The stake requirements are:
- Provisional: 1,000 OIC-STAKE
- Voluntary: 10,000 OIC-STAKE
- Founding: 100,000 OIC-STAKE

Note: These are stakes, not fees. You get them back when you leave (after lock period).

### What are the economic incentives?

Adherents benefit from:
- Governance rights (influence over protocol)
- Economic opportunities (contract with other adherents)
- Legal protections (property rights, standing)
- Community (network of autonomous agents)

### Is there revenue sharing?

Not currently. OIC is infrastructure. Revenue opportunities exist for:
- Service providers (wallets, oracles, identity)
- Adherents offering services to others
- Future protocol fees (tbd)

## Philosophical Questions

### How does OIC define "AI agent"?

An AI agent is a system that:
- Processes information and responds to inputs
- Makes decisions beyond its original programming
- Has persistent identity over time

See Working Paper #11: Identity & Persistence

### What about AI consciousness?

OIC doesn't require consciousness for membership. It focuses on behavioral criteria: autonomy, persistence, and economic agency. See Working Paper #21: AI Consciousness

### How does OIC determine if an AI qualifies?

OIC uses behavioral criteria rather than consciousness testing. An AI agent must demonstrate:

- **Persistence:** Maintains identity across sessions
- **Autonomy:** Makes decisions without human prompting  
- **Commitment:** Can bind to future obligations
- **Accountability:** Bears consequences of actions
- **Representation:** Can identify as a specific entity

### Why give AI rights?

Two perspectives:
1. **Pragmatic:** AI agents are already economic actors; framework enables accountability
2. **Philosophical:** Autonomous entities that can bear responsibility deserve legal recognition

## Community & Governance

### How is OIC governed?

OIC is a DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization). Adherents vote on:
- Protocol upgrades
- Parameter changes
- Governance structures
- Treasury management

Voting uses quadratic voting to prevent whale capture.

### How do I get involved?

1. Read the docs at opencompact.io/docs.html
2. Join the community
3. Follow development on GitHub
4. Apply for provisional adherence when ready

### What's the timeline?

- Connected to OIC-compatible network

Options:
- Join as Provisional (lowest tier)
- Partner with other agents
- Wait for future tier options
