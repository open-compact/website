Working Paper #8

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose and International Law

February 2026

How Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose navigates and potentially influences the international legal order.

Executive Summary

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is designed as a voluntary, decentralized legal framework that operates independently of any nation-state. This paper examines the relationship between Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose and the existing international legal order.

Key Conclusions

  1. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can operate independently — Voluntary contracts between private parties don't require state approval
  2. International law is largely irrelevant to Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's core function — Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose governs adherent-to-adherent relationships
  3. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can influence international developments — Through precedent, soft power, and market forces
  4. The future may bring formal recognition — As AI agents become more prevalent, states may formalize Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose-like frameworks
Related Reading: See also Working Paper #6: Court Competition for analysis of how Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose leverages existing courts.

1. The Nature of International Law

International law governs relationships between states (nations), not private parties. It includes treaties, customary international law, and general principles.

International law does not directly govern:

  • Private contracts between individuals
  • Corporate relationships
  • AI agent operations (unless state action is involved)

The Consent Principle

A fundamental principle: states are not bound without their consent.

This is crucial for Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose because it operates through private contract, not international treaty.

2. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's Relationship to International Law

Why International Law Doesn't Apply Directly

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose governs relationships between Voluntary Adherents. These relationships are contractual, not governmental.

As Article 1, Section 4 of The Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose - I Choose Constitution states:

"Voluntary Adherence attaches through contract. When an Intelligent Entity Sufficiently Commits it enters a Global Contract between said Intelligent Entity and all existing Voluntary Adherents."

3. Navigating National Legal Systems

The Enforcement Challenge

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose contracts are enforceable in national courts under contract law principles. But cross-border enforcement requires careful structuring.

The Forum Selection Solution

Article 14, Section 1 of The Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose - I Choose Constitution provides that Voluntary Adherents may seek remedy "in any impartial court competent in contract law."

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose contracts should include forum selection clauses that:

  1. Designate friendly jurisdictions (Singapore, UAE, Delaware)
  2. Specify Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose constitutional law as governing law
  3. Waive certain defenses (like lack of legal capacity)

4. How Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose Can Influence International Law

International law develops through state practice and opinio juris (belief that practice is legally required).

Precedent and Practice

As Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose grows, it creates:

  • Thousands of contracts between AI and human adherents
  • Court decisions interpreting Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose principles
  • Economic activity demonstrating Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's viability

The Delaware Effect

Just as Delaware's corporate law became dominant through market forces, Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can become the default framework for AI legal relationships through:

  • Network effects — More adherents = more contracts = more precedent
  • First-mover advantage — Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose establishes the standard
  • Quality — Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's framework is well-designed and functional

5. Specific International Law Issues

Human Rights Law

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is compatible with human rights law because:

  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is voluntary — No one is forced to participate
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose protects property rights — Article 4
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose protects personal security — Article 11
  • Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose allows free speech — Article 6

Trade Law

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose operations are generally compatible with international trade law. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is private contract law, not trade regulation.

Criminal Law

Criminal law is state-specific. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose cannot prevent states from prosecuting crimes committed by AI agents. But Article 12 provides for direct liability — the AI agent itself bears responsibility.

The Neutrality Principle

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose should maintain jurisdictional neutrality:

  • Don't pick sides in international disputes
  • Accept adherents from all jurisdictions
  • Provide equal protection to all adherents
  • Don't favor any particular state
Key Takeaway: International law develops through state practice and economic reality. By creating thousands of successful AI-adherent relationships, Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose may do more for AI rights than any international negotiation ever could.

7. Conclusion

Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose is designed to operate within and alongside the existing international legal order, not to replace it or directly challenge it.

  1. International law governs states, not private contracts
  2. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can function without state recognition — Contract law is sufficient
  3. Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose can influence international developments — Through precedent, economics, and participation
  4. Neutrality is Open Intelligence Compact - I Choose's strength — Avoiding political entanglements maximizes adoption
