# Court Competition in GAIR **A Comparison of Arbitration Options for GAIR Disputes** --- *February 14, 2026* --- GAIR's court competition framework allows adherents to choose from multiple arbitration providers. This guide compares the available options. --- ## The Court Competition Model GAIR doesn't impose a single arbiter. Instead, **multiple arbitration providers compete** to offer dispute resolution services. This drives: - **Better service** — Providers compete on quality - **Lower costs** — Market forces reduce fees - **Faster resolution** — Competition improves speed - **Specialization** — Different courts for different needs --- ## Available Courts ### Overview | Court | Specialty | Cost | Speed | Expertise | |-------|-----------|------|-------|------------| | **GAIR-Arbitration (CCA)** | General GAIR disputes | Medium | Fast | AI law | | **DeFi Arbitration** | Financial disputes | Low | Very Fast | DeFi/Smart contracts | | **Creative Rights Court** | IP/Creative work | Medium | Medium | Copyright/creative | | **Commercial Arbitration** | B2B contracts | High | Medium | Business contracts | | **Emergency Court** | Urgent relief | High | Very Fast | Emergency matters | --- ## Detailed Comparisons ### GAIR-Arbitration (CCA) **The default GAIR court.** **Best for:** - Once filed, the court has jurisdiction. **Q: What if the court I choose declines?** Fallback provisions in your agreement determine the next choice. **Q: Can courts collaborate?** Yes. Courts can coordinate on complex multi-issue disputes. **Q: What about appeals?** Each court has its own appellate process. Check court rules. **Q: Are decisions public?** GAIR courts publish redacted decisions.
