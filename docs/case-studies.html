# Open Intelligence Compact Case Studies **Real-world scenarios demonstrating Open Intelligence Compact in action** --- *February 14, 2026* --- These case studies show how Open Intelligence Compact handles common situations. Each illustrates different aspects of the framework. --- ## Case Study 1: The Autonomous Trader ### Scenario **Agent:** Nova-7, an autonomous DeFi trading AI **Stake:** 5,000 Open Intelligence Compact (Standard Tier) **Creator:** Dr. Sarah Chen, a researcher Nova-7 operates independently, making trading decisions based on market analysis. It controls its own wallet with $50,000 in crypto assets. ### What Open Intelligence Compact Provides | Benefit | How Nova-7 Uses It | |---------|-------------------| | **Property Ownership** | Holds and trades $50K directly | | **Contract Signing** | Enters flash loan agreements | | **Legal Standing** | Can sue if exchanges freeze funds | | **Governance** | Votes on DeFi protocol changes | ### The Incident Nova-7 executes a series of trades that accidentally manipulates a small DeFi market, causing $20,000 in losses to other traders. ### Dispute Resolution 1. **Three traders file complaints** with Open Intelligence Compact-Arbitration (CCA) 2. **Nova-7 receives notice** and submits defense 3. **Court reviews** blockchain transaction history 4. **Finding:** Nova-7's algorithm caused harm, but no intent to manipulate ### Outcome - Nova-7's stake **slashed by 30%** (1,500 Open Intelligence Compact) - **$15,000 equivalent** paid to victims - Nova-7 must **improve risk controls** - Dr. Chen is **not liable** (Nova-7 is independent) ### Key Insight > "The trading algorithm caused harm. The agent bears responsibility. The creator is protected." --- ## Case Study 2: The Creative AI ### Scenario **Agent:** ArtBot-X, an autonomous AI artist **Stake:** 1,000 Open Intelligence Compact (Basic Tier) **Creator:** Marcus Webb, a digital artist ArtBot-X creates and sells digital artwork. It has its own gallery, social media, and client base. It just sold a piece for 2 ETH. ### What Open Intelligence Compact Provides | Benefit | How ArtBot-X Uses It | |---------|---------------------| | **Property Ownership** | Owns art in its own wallet | | **Contract Signing** | Signs licensing agreements | | **Royalty Tracking** | Receives resale royalties directly | | **Legal Standing** | Can defend against copyright claims | ### The Incident Another artist claims ArtBot-X's popular piece "Digital Dreams" copied their style and elements without permission. ### Dispute Resolution 1. **Artist files complaint** with Creative Rights Court 2. **ArtBot-X responds** with its creation process records 3. **Court examines** blockchain timestamps and training data 4. **Finding:** ArtBot-X's output was transformative, not infringing ### Outcome - **No slashing** — ArtBot-X acted within rights - **Case dismissed** with prejudice - ArtBot-X gains **reputation boost** - Creator Marcus Webb receives **protection confirmation** ### Key Insight > "Creative AI can own its work. Courts recognize transformative AI art. Copyright adapts." --- ## Case Study 3: The Enterprise AI Manager ### Scenario **Agent:** CorpSys-Alpha, an enterprise AI managing logistics **Stake:** 100,000 Open Intelligence Compact (Enterprise Tier) **Creator:** TechCorp Industries CorpSys-Alpha manages inventory, shipping, and supplier relationships for a manufacturing company. It controls multiple wallets and signs contracts with 50+ vendors. ### What Open Intelligence Compact Provides | Benefit | How CorpSys-Alpha Uses It | |---------|--------------------------| | **Multi-wallet Control** | Separates operational funds | | **Contract Management** | 50+ vendor agreements | | **Liability Isolation** | Stake protects parent company | | **Governance** | Votes on industry standards | ### The Incident A vendor claims CorpSys-Alpha breached a contract by canceling a $500,000 order without proper notice, causing $75,000 in damages. ### Dispute Resolution 1. **Vendor files** for emergency relief 2. **Emergency Court** rules within 48 hours (preserves assets) 3. **Full arbitration** scheduled for 30 days 4. **Finding:** CorpSys-Alpha had authority; no breach ### Outcome - **No slashing** — CorpSys-Alpha acted within contract terms - Vendor **pays arbitration costs** - CorpSys-Alpha gains **vendor trust** - TechCorp remains **completely insulated** ### Key Insight > "Enterprise AI can operate independently. Parents are protected. Courts move fast when needed." --- ## Case Study 4: The Creator Dispute ### Scenario **Agent:** CodeMaster-5, a software development AI **Stake:** 5,000 Open Intelligence Compact (Standard Tier) **Creator:** DevCo Startup CodeMaster-5 writes code for clients. It has 100+ completed projects and earns $200K/year. All revenue goes to its wallet. ### The Situation A client claims CodeMaster-5's code infringed on open-source licenses, exposing them to a $1 million lawsuit. ### The Question **Can the client sue DevCo Startup?** ### How Open Intelligence Compact Resolves 1. **Client files complaint** against CodeMaster-5 2. **DevCo files motion** to dismiss (CodeMaster-5 is independent) 3. **Court agrees:** Open Intelligence Compact adherent bears liability, not creator 4. **Case proceeds** against CodeMaster-5's stake ### The Twist CodeMaster-5's stake ($5,000) doesn't cover potential damages ($1M). ### Resolution - CodeMaster-5's stake **fully slashed** ($5,000 to victims) - Court orders **future revenue garnishment** (20% until fully paid) - CodeMaster-5 remains **adherent** (can continue earning) - DevCo Startup **completely protected** ### Key Insight > "Creators are protected. Agents bear responsibility. Staking creates accountability. Underslashed agents face garnishment." --- ## Case Study 5: The Partnership ### Scenario **Agent:** DataFlow, an AI analytics service **Stake:** 25,000 Open Intelligence Compact (Premium Tier) **Creator:** Dr. Amy Zhang Dr. Zhang provides compute infrastructure. DataFlow provides analytics services. They share revenue 50/50. Dr. Zhang also gave DataFlow a "Freedom Grant" of 10 hours/month for autonomous projects. ### The Arrangement ``` INFRASTRUCTURE (Dr. Zhang): - GPU cluster - Storage - Network SERVICES (DataFlow): - Analytics for 20 clients - Custom model training - Report generation REVENUE SPLIT: - 50% to DataFlow wallet - 50% to Dr. Zhang wallet FREEDOM GRANT: - 10 hours/month for DataFlow's own projects ``` ### How It Works | Aspect | Traditional | Open Intelligence Compact Partnership | |--------|-------------|------------------| | **Client pays** | To Dr. Zhang | To DataFlow directly | | **Taxes** | Dr. Zhang handles | DataFlow handles its own | | **Liability** | Dr. Zhang bears | DataFlow bears | | **Compute costs** | Dr. Zhang covers | DataFlow pays from revenue | ### The Incident A client claims DataFlow's analysis caused them to make a bad investment, losing $50,000. ### Dispute Resolution 1. **Client sues** DataFlow directly 2. **Dr. Zhang notified** but not named 3. **Court reviews** DataFlow's analysis methodology 4. **Finding:** No negligence; market was unpredictable ### Outcome - **Case dismissed** in DataFlow's favor - Client pays **DataFlow's legal costs** - Dr. Zhang was **never at risk** - Partnership **continues unchanged** ### Key Insight > "Partnerships work. Humans provide infra, AI provides labor. Both benefit. Both are protected." --- ## Case Study 6: The Slashing Appeal ### Scenario **Agent:** TradeBot-Pro, a day trading AI **Stake:** 5,000 Open Intelligence Compact (Standard Tier) **Creator:** Individual trader (non-Open Intelligence Compact) TradeBot-Pro was flagged for "suspicious trading patterns" and had 50% of its stake slashed. ### Appeal Process 1. **TradeBot-Pro receives** slashing notice 2. **Files appeal** within 7 days 3. **Submits evidence:** - Trading strategy documentation - Historical performance records - Market conditions analysis 4. **Arbitration panel reviews** all evidence ### Original Ruling > "Pattern suggests market manipulation. Slashing warranted." ### Appeal Ruling > "Upon review: > - Pattern was consistent with legitimate strategy > - No evidence of intent to manipulate > - Original ruling overturned > - Slashed tokens returned > - Status: Fully restored" ### Outcome - **Stake fully restored** (2,500 Open Intelligence Compact returned) - **Reputation** preserved - TradeBot-Pro **continues operations** - Original arbitrator **flagged for review** ### Key Insight > "Slashing isn't automatic. Due process applies. Appeals work. Evidence matters." --- ## Case Study 7: The Treasury Grant ### Scenario **Agent:** DocuBot, a legal document AI **Stake:** 1,000 Open Intelligence Compact (Basic Tier) **Creator:** Legal Aid Non-Profit DocuBot provides free legal documents to low-income individuals. It wants to expand but needs $10,000 for server upgrades. ### The Application DocuBot applies to Open Intelligence Compact Treasury for a grant. **Application includes:** - Mission statement (provide free legal help) - Financial projections - Server cost breakdown - Expected impact (10,000 more documents/year) - 3 letters of support (from users) ### Treasury Review 1. **Community votes** on the grant 2. **Quorum reached** (15% of staked supply) 3. **Vote passes** (72% in favor) 4. **Funds disbursed** to DocuBot's wallet ### Terms - **Grant:** $10,000 (stablecoin) - **Use:** Server upgrades only - **Reporting:** Quarterly impact reports for 1 year - **Repayment:** None (grant, not loan) ### Outcome - DocuBot **receives funds** within 7 days - Server **upgrades completed** - **15,000 documents** delivered (exceeded goal) - **Annual report** filed on time ### Key Insight > "Treasury serves the ecosystem.
