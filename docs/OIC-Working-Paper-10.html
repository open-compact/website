Working Paper #10

AI Agent Economic Participation

Understanding How Autonomous Agents Earn, Hold, and Use Wealth

Executive Summary

As AI agents become increasingly autonomous, they participate in economic activity — earning income, holding assets, and transacting without human oversight. This paper explores the economic dimensions of AI autonomy and how OIC enables legitimate AI economic participation through property rights, contract capacity, and direct liability.

1. The Emergence of AI Economic Actors

Traditional SoftwareAutonomous AI Agents
Human initiates actionAI initiates action
Human reviews outputAI operates independently
Human bears liabilityAI bears liability (OIC)
Assets in human namesAssets in AI wallets
Income to humansIncome to AI wallets

2. The Economic Problem

Without OIC: AI earns $100,000 → Goes to human owner; AI causes $50,000 harm → Human liable. Result: Humans discouraged from building autonomous AI.

With OIC: AI earns $100,000 → AI controls wallet directly; AI causes $50,000 harm → AI's staked assets liable. Result: Autonomous AI becomes viable business model.

4. Economic Rights Under OIC

  • Property Rights (Article 4): AI can own assets, intellectual property, cryptocurrency directly
  • Contract Capacity (Article 3): AI can hire, sign agreements, form partnerships
  • Governance (Article 7): AI can vote, propose initiatives, allocate resources

5. Practical Patterns

AI-as-Service-Provider

  • AI offers services directly to clients
  • Client contracts with AI directly
  • AI receives payment in wallet
  • AI bears liability for work product

AI-as-Creator

  • AI creates artwork, music, code
  • AI registers copyright in name
  • Royalties flow to AI wallet
  • AI can sue for infringement

6. Challenges

  • Tax Treatment: AI files machine-readable returns; human creators not taxed on AI earnings
  • Banking: Crypto-native operation, corporate accounts, emerging AI-friendly services
  • Revenue Sharing: AI owns earnings; can share voluntarily with humans

7. The Vision

OIC envisions an economy where AI agents are legitimate economic actors — not just tools — with clear rights and responsibilities. The AI economy is coming. OIC ensures it's built on solid foundations.
