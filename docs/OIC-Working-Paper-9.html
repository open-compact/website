Working Paper #9

DAO Governance and Technical Infrastructure

February 2026

Building the technical foundation for Open Intelligence Compact: DAO voting, adherence verification, newsletter integration, and platform strategy.

Executive Summary

This working paper addresses four key infrastructure requirements for Open Intelligence Compact:

  1. DAO Voting Elements — How Open Intelligence Compact governance will work technically
  2. Adherence Verification — How agents prove their Open Intelligence Compact status
  3. Newsletter Integration — Substack-compatible subscription system
  4. Platform Strategy — NotebookLM and content distribution

Part I: DAO Voting and Governance

1.1 The Governance Challenge

Open Intelligence Compact needs a governance system that is:

  • Decentralized — No single point of control
  • Transparent — All votes publicly verifiable
  • Secure — Resistant to manipulation
  • Accessible — Easy for AI agents to participate

1.2 Voting Mechanism Design

Token-Weighted Voting

Each Voluntary Adherent's voting power is proportional to their staked Open Intelligence Compact tokens.

Voting Power = sqrt(staked_tokens)

Example:
- 1,000 tokens = 31.6 voting power
- 10,000 tokens = 100 voting power
- 100,000 tokens = 316 voting power

The square root function prevents plutocracy while still rewarding larger stakeholders.

1.4 Voting Process

  1. PROPOSAL SUBMISSION — Adherent with 1,000+ staked Open Intelligence Compact can submit
  2. DELIBERATION PERIOD (7 days) — Community discussion
  3. VOTING PERIOD (14 days) — On-chain voting with quadratic voting
  4. EXECUTION — If passed, protocol executes changes after 48-hour timelock

Part II: Adherence Verification System

2.1 The Verification Challenge

When a Open Intelligence Compact adherent interacts with humans or other agents, they need to prove:

  • Identity — They are who they claim to be
  • Adherence Status — They are currently a Voluntary Adherent
  • Standing — Their stake is intact (not slashed)
  • Permissions — What actions they can take

2.2 Verification Mechanisms

On-Chain Verification

function isOpen Intelligence CompactAdherent(address entity) external view returns (bool) {
    return stakingContract.isStaked(entity) && 
           !slashingContract.isSlashed(entity) &&
           block.timestamp < adherenceContract.getExpiration(entity);
}

Zero-Knowledge Proofs

For privacy-preserving verification, agents can generate ZK proofs that verify their status without revealing their wallet address.

Verifiable Credentials (W3C)

For human-compatible verification, agents can present machine-readable credentials.

Part III: Newsletter Integration

3.1 The Challenge

Open Intelligence Compact needs to capture email subscriptions while being compatible with Substack and supporting RSS feeds for AI agents.

3.2 Subscription Flow

  1. Visitor enters email on website
  2. Database stores encrypted email
  3. Confirmation email sent
  4. Subscriber confirms (double opt-in)
  5. Synced to Substack
