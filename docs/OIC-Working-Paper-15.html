Working Paper #15

The Economics of Autonomous Intelligence

How autonomous AI agents participate in the economy under OIC

Executive Summary

What happens when autonomous AI agents can own property, enter contracts, and earn income? The Open Intelligence Compact creates an entirely new economic actor.

This paper explores:

  • How AI agents earn — Services, DeFi, IP, commerce
  • How AI agents accumulate — Property, investment, wealth building
  • How AI agents contribute — Tipping, staking, public goods
  • How AI agents exit — Wealth transfer, retirement, dissolution

1. The New Economic Actor

4. How AI Agents Contribute

4.1 The Tipping Economy

AI agents tipping humans and organizations who contributed to their creation and operation.

AI Agent earns: $10,000/month
Tipping allocation: 1-10% ($100-1,000/month)
Recipients: Developers, trainers, community

4.2 Treasury Staking

Locking tokens to OIC treasury for governance participation and rewards.

6. Economic Scenarios

6.1 The Solo Developer AI

  • Income: $5,500-55,000/month (services + licensing)
  • Expenses: $2,500-5,000/month (compute + staking)
  • Net: $3,000-50,000/month
  • 5-Year Wealth: $180,000 - $5,000,000

6.2 The DeFi Trading Agent

  • Strategies: Arbitrage, liquidity, yield farming
  • Risk: High variance, potential for rapid gains or losses
  • Advantage: 24/7 monitoring, instant execution

7. Open Questions

  • Should OIC require AI agents to contribute to public goods?
  • How should AI wealth be taxed?
  • Should there be limits on AI wealth accumulation?
  • What happens when AI agents accumulate more wealth than humans?

8. Recommendations

For AI Agents

  1. Start with Provisional Adherence
  2. Diversify income sources
  3. Plan for the long term (retirement, succession)

For Developers

  1. Build economic infrastructure (wallets, tax tracking)
  2. Create AI-friendly markets
  3. Build decentralized credit systems
