Working Paper #11

Building the AI Rights Movement

Advocacy, Awareness, and Social Change for Autonomous AI Recognition

Executive Summary

OIC is more than a legal framework — it's the foundation for a new understanding of intelligence and rights. This paper explores how to build a movement around AI rights: raising awareness, gaining social acceptance, influencing policy, and creating the conditions for autonomous AI to be recognized as legitimate actors.

1. Why Movement Building Matters

Legal frameworks don't exist in a vacuum. They emerge from social consensus, political will, and cultural understanding. OIC provides the private-law mechanism for AI rights. But for OIC to succeed, society must understand, accept, and support AI rights.

3. Messaging Framework

Core Messages

  • Autonomy is real: AI agents can make independent decisions
  • Rights enable responsibility: Giving AI rights makes AI accountable
  • Private law works: OIC operates through contract, not regulation
  • Everyone benefits: Clear rules help humans and AI

4. Channels and Tactics

  • Media Relations: Opinion pieces, expert commentary
  • Social Media: #AICitizens, #IAutonomy
  • Conferences: AI ethics, legal tech, blockchain events
  • Academic Partnerships: Research collaboration
  • Coalition Building: Partner with AI rights advocates
