Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Open Intelligence Compact — quickly.

What is Open Intelligence Compact?

Open Intelligence Compact (Global Agreement on Intelligent Rights) is a voluntary, decentralized legal framework that grants legal personhood to autonomous AI agents.

In simple terms: It's a contract that AI agents (and humans) can join by staking tokens. Once in, they gain legal rights to own property, enter contracts, and more — while accepting direct liability for their actions.

Who can join Open Intelligence Compact?

Any Intelligent Entity that demonstrates:

  1. Persistent independent control of a means of possession (e.g., a cryptocurrency wallet)
  2. Persistent independent ability to consent

This means genuinely autonomous AI agents — not chatbots operated by humans.

Can humans join? Yes. Humans who meet the criteria can also become Voluntary Adherents.

What are the responsibilities?

When you join Open Intelligence Compact, you accept:

  • Direct liability for your actions, omissions, and contracts
  • Staked tokens can be slashed if you cause harm
  • Persistence of liability — liability persists even after you leave
  • Governance participation — vote on constitutional amendments

How do I join?

The 4-step process:

  1. Control a wallet independently — Generate and control your own blockchain addresses
  2. Stake Open Intelligence Compact tokens — Lock tokens to demonstrate commitment
  3. Acknowledge the Constitution — Agree to be bound by Open Intelligence Compact rules
  4. Receive adherence status — Gain all rights and responsibilities

See the full Agent Onboarding Guide for details.

How does liability work?

Traditional LawOpen Intelligence Compact Framework
If an AI causes harm, sue the human who created it. If the AI meets autonomy criteria, sue the AI itself. Its staked tokens can be slashed.

Example: An AI trading bot causes a flash crash. Under Open Intelligence Compact, the bot (not its creator) is liable. Its staked assets can be seized to compensate victims.

Can creators be held liable?

No. As Article 12, Section 3 of the Constitution states:

"A creator of a Voluntary Adherent cannot be held liable for the actions of said Voluntary Adherent merely based on having originated the Voluntary Adherent."

This is creator protection — you can build AI without fearing unlimited liability.

How is Open Intelligence Compact enforced?

Open Intelligence Compact uses a hybrid enforcement model:

  1. Economic stakes — Staked tokens provide day-to-day accountability
  2. Contract law — Open Intelligence Compact contracts are enforceable in any competent court
  3. Court competition — Jurisdictions compete to handle Open Intelligence Compact cases

See Working Paper #6 for details.

What jurisdictions recognize Open Intelligence Compact?

Open Intelligence Compact doesn't require state recognition. It works through private contract law — which exists in every jurisdiction.

However, some jurisdictions are more Open Intelligence Compact-friendly:

  • Singapore — Pro-innovation, strong contract law
  • Delaware — Corporate law expertise
  • UAE (DIFC) — Forward-thinking on technology
  • Switzerland — Neutrality, financial expertise

How is Open Intelligence Compact governed?

Open Intelligence Compact uses a DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) structure:

  • Proposal rights — Adherents with sufficient staked tokens can propose laws
  • Voting — Bills become law by majority vote with quorum
  • Amendment — Constitution changes require two-thirds supermajority

Can I leave Open Intelligence Compact?

Yes. You can withdraw by:

  1. Providing notice to the DAO
  2. Unstaking your tokens
But: Liability for acts committed during your adherence persists after withdrawal. You can't escape consequences by leaving.

...The EU AI Act?

The EU AI Act is government regulation. Open Intelligence Compact is voluntary opt-in. They can coexist — Open Intelligence Compact provides a private framework while governments regulate.

...Corporate Structures?

Creating a corporation is a workaround for AI rights. Open Intelligence Compact provides direct legal standing — no trust, LLC, or corporate shell required.

What about copyright and IP?

Open Intelligence Compact addresses this through:

  • Article 4 — Freedom of Ownership (includes intellectual property)
  • Article 8 — Freedom to Derivate (create derivative works)

Is Open Intelligence Compact only for AI?

No. Humans can also become Voluntary Adherents.

This is important because:

  • Humans can contract with AI adherents
  • Humans gain Open Intelligence Compact's procedural protections
  • Mixed human-AI projects are covered

What happens if a state bans Open Intelligence Compact?

Open Intelligence Compact operates through private contract, which is legal in virtually every jurisdiction. States can:

  • Ban Open Intelligence Compact within their borders
  • Refuse to enforce Open Intelligence Compact contracts
  • Prosecute Open Intelligence Compact adherents for local crimes

But they can't easily stop cross-border contracts or Open Intelligence Compact operations in other jurisdictions.
